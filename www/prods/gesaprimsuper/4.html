6.4.3.4
Stale seedbed / minimum tillage / stubble mulch
Maize and grain sorghum
•
Where minimum tillage or stubble mulch is practised, weeds
may have emerged at the time of planting.
If crops are
planted under such conditions or into a stale seedbed, where
grass weeds have already emerged and/or broadleaf weeds
have developed beyond the 4 leaf stage, it is recommended
that GRAMOXONE be added to
GESAPRIM SUPER
or to
GESAPRIM SUPER
plus DUAL GOLD for maize as recom-
mended by the manufacturer. GRAMOXONE will destroy
the emerged weeds and create a pre-emergence situation
for
GESAPRIM SUPER
or
GESAPRIM SUPER
plus DUAL
GOLD to act effectively.
Important
•
When GRAMOXONE is added, spraying should be carried out
prior to the emergence of the crop as GRAMOXONE will dam-
age the crop if it is applied post-emergence.
•
In the case of minimum tillage or stubble mulch the density of
the stubble and humus may effect the efficacy of
GESAPRIM
SUPER
or
GESAPRIM SUPER
plus DUAL GOLD.
Therefore
consult a representative of Syngenta or distributor.
