DIRECTIONS FOR USE:
Use only as indicated.
1.
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
should only be applied to
actively growing weeds that are not dormant or under tem-
perature, moisture or any other stress. Rain or irrigation a few
days prior to a
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
application
ensures that weeds are actively growing, resulting in optimum
efficacy. Weeds growing during a stress period might seem
small, however it could have completed its biological growth
phase and should therefore be treated as a mature weed
when selecting dosage rates. The same apply to plants that
has been grazed, cut, burned of even sprayed once with
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
or any other herbicide.
2.
Use only clean water with no suspended soil particles in the
spray mixture.
3.
Always ensure that spray equipment is clean, free from all sedi-
ments, rust or dust and correctly calibrated before spraying.
4.
Use a low spray pressure (100 - 200 kPa) and the correct
nozzles to avoid spray drift.
5.
Ensure even coverage of all weeds under application and
apply to incipient runoff.
6.
When using
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
for chemical
seedbed preparation for transplanted tomatoes, tobacco
or any transplanted crop with green, soft stems, allow a
minimum of 14 days between application and transplanting
of seedlings.
7.
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
is actively absorbed through
immature bark and leaves of most plants and trees. Contact
with immature bark, such as in trees younger than three
years, can result in serious localised or translocated damage.
Therefore contact with leaves, green or immature bark and
fruit of desired plants, whether direct or by spray drift, must
be avoided. Always make sure that only undesired plants are
treated.
8.
Do not spray onto pruned vines or fruit trees until wounds
have sealed properly.
9.
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
is a non-selective systemic
herbicide and is only active when applied to the foliage and
green bark of plants. The visible effect of
TOUCHDOWN Forte
HITECH
on treated foliage usually appears at 7 - 10 days after
treatment but may vary according to weather conditions.
10.
Rain or irrigation within 3 hours of application can reduce the
efficacy of
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
.
11.
Do not spray on weed foliage covered with a layer of dust. In
these situations apply after recent rain.
12.
Do not apply
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
to weeds that
are covered with dew, or in a dormant growth phase or under
stress conditions e.g. moisture, heat or cold stess.
13.
Do not apply
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
when the atmo-
spheric conditions are less favourable e.g. the humidity are
less than 40% or the wind speed are more than 10 km/h.
14.
Pesticide run-off onto
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
-
treated weeds under trees in orchards can adversely affect
the herbicide’s activity. Do NOT spray other pesticides within
12 hours of a
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
application.
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
has no pre-emergence
acti vity, therefore repeat applications are necessary (when
applied on its own) to control weeds germinating from
seed. Ensure that target weeds are fully exposed to the
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
spray.
15.
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
can be applied on glypho-
sate tolerant cotton, soybean and maize plants. Any individual
plants that do not contain the glyphosate tolerant gene would
die when sprayed with glyphosate based products e.g.
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH.
16.
TOUCHDOWN Forte HITECH
will not control volunteer
glyphosate-tolerant crops.
17.
Inconsistent and variable control of weeds even at high rates
may be experienced due to a number of reasons namely
a) drought stress
b) cold or heat stress
c)
plants with waxy layers e.g.

e)
acquired resistance due to the repeated use of glyphosate
e.g.
Lolium
spp. and

(confirmed

f)
poor coverage and penetration of exposed leaves e.g.
Argemone subfusiformis,
g) plants with bulbs and tubers e.g.
Cyperus
spp.
,
h)
inconsistent relationship between above soil parts and
below soil parts e.g.
Conyza bonariensis
after dry periods
or growth during the winter.
i)
poor water quality.
